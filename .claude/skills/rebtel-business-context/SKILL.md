# Rebtel Business Context

## What Rebtel Does
Rebtel is a Swedish telecom company (est. 2006) that helps immigrant communities stay connected with family and friends across borders. The core value prop: affordable, high-quality international communication and financial services — no internet required for calling.

**Vision:** Two worlds connected. A future with less borders and friction, where realities, perspectives, and dreams are seamlessly connected.

**Mission:** Help friends and family in different countries stay in touch and support each other by delivering the world's most relevant cross-border solutions.

## Users
Rebtel serves a diaspora/immigrant audience. The typical user lives in a developed country (the "A-side") and communicates with or sends value to someone in a developing country (the "B-side").

- **A-side (sender):** Origin user. Lives in US, Europe, etc. Has smartphone, internet, and purchasing power.
- **B-side (receiver):** Receiving user. Often in a developing nation. May have limited or no internet, expensive data. Receives calls, top-ups, or remittances.

**Strategic focus:** Profitable growth. A LTV/CAC over 2

## Market & Competition

### The Market
Rebtel operates in the "Diaspora Services" market — cross-border connectivity for immigrant communities. Two pillars: international calling and top-ups (MTU/utilities/gift cards). The market is fragmented with regional champions and no single global winner. The major indirect threat is free OTT apps (WhatsApp, Viber, FaceTime) which have been eroding calling revenue industry-wide since ~2012.

### Key Competitors

**Boss Revolution** (IDT Corporation) — ~4x Rebtel's size. ~$670M revenue, ~300 people, 30K+ US retail stores. Dominant in US → LatAm and US → Africa corridors. Offers calling, MTU, remittance. Strengths: ease of use, call value, high brand recognition. Weaknesses: poor customer support, payment failures, confusing app UX.

**Ding** — Main MTU competitor. ~$150-200M revenue, ~300 people. Best-in-class profitability (fees up to 45% on top-ups). Three business lines: Ding for Consumer (direct competitor), Ding for Business (Rebtel buys MTUs from Ding to resell), Ding for Operators. Strong in North America, Europe, Middle East. Strengths: fast delivery, localized payments, loyalty program, strong onboarding. Weaknesses: intransparent/hidden fees, non-deliverables.

**Libon** (Orange spin-off) — ~$50-70M revenue, ~70 people. Strong in Europe (France, Spain, Italy), never cracked the US. Biggest diaspora: Malians. Combines offline and online GTM. Strengths: per-second billing, customer support, GTM approach. Weaknesses: call quality, app crashes, account blocks.

**Yolla** — Small Estonian player. ~$12M revenue, ~10 people. Widest service range: calling, SMS, MTU. Targets African diasporas (Nigeria, Eritrea, Kenya, Ghana). Strengths: multi-currency display, broad offering. Weaknesses: caller ID issues, payment failures.

**Cuballama** — Cuba-only niche. 11-50 people. Hyper-focused: calling, grocery delivery (Mercado), parcels (Envios), top-ups. Highest rated competitor (4.8). Strengths: deep local service offering, simple UX, recargas top-of-mind. Weaknesses: narrow market focus.

### Competitive Insights
- **MTU loyalty is near-zero** — customers shop on price across providers. Rebtel must either improve unit economics or crack retention to win long-term.
- **Two winning MTU strategies:** hyper-localization (Cuballama model) or well-rounded global experience with localized payments (Ding model).
- **Calling table stakes:** clear and stable calls. Get it right = promoter. Get it wrong = churn. Ease of use and convenience are the key drivers of appreciation.
- **Payment failures are universally toxic** — especially when coupled with fraud accusations or account blocks.
- **Customer support** is the #2 complaint driver across all competitors, but also a trust-builder when done right (timely, relevant, right language, right channel).
- **Rebtel's perceived strengths:** high-quality calls, fee-free top-ups, affordability. **Perceived weaknesses:** customer support, account blocks, charges without completed calls.

## Core Products

### 1. International Calling (majority of revenue, declining)
- Affordable international calls routed through local phone lines (PSTN), not internet — so quality is high and no data is needed.
- Plans include unlimited calling to specific countries (e.g., Mexico), minute bundles, and pay-as-you-go via Rebtel Credits.
- Two call types: **MXP** (calls via data/wifi in-app) and **PSTN** (calls via local phone lines).
- Requires iOS or Android app to use
- Calling revenue is steadily declining.
- Strategy: be best-in-class on quality and UX, accept second position to WhatsApp, build the "Of course I have Rebtel as well" proposition.

### 2. Mobile Top Up / MTU (fastest growing)
- Send mobile airtime to someone abroad (e.g., your mother running out of minutes).
- Can be one-time or subscription (auto-renew every X days).
- Revenue is growing fast — strategic goal is for MTU to eventually surpass Calling in revenue.
- Strategy: shift from price-led commodity to relationship-led service by emphasizing the sender-recipient connection.

## Company Strategies
1. **Best in class** in current products — MTU, Calling, and UX across all touchpoints. Easy to join, simple to use, unthinkable to leave.
2. **Activate early:** Focus on making new users highly active in their first two months.
3. **New market entry:** System for quickly entering and evaluating new markets.
4. **Localization & new services:** Enable local relevance and expand the product portfolio.
5. **Calling positioning:** Best-in-class quality/UX while embracing second position to WhatsApp.
6. **MTU differentiation:** Move from price-led commodity to relationship-led service by emphasizing the sender-recipient connection.


## Key Terminology

### User Lifecycle
- **Rebtel User:** Any Active or Paying User.
- **Active User:** Made a call, MTU in the current period (outgoing events only — received calls don't count).
- **Paying User:** Made a paid transaction (calling, burn-in, MTU,). Precise rules vary by product — see business definitions.
- **NPU (New Paying User):** First time a user qualifies as Paying. Counted only once regardless of service type.
- **Recurring User:** Active in both current and previous period.
- **Churned User:** Active in previous period, inactive in current period.
- **Reactivated User:** Was churned, now active again.
- **Trial User:** Uses a Trial Product.
- **Barred/Unbarred User:** Account deactivated / reactivated.

### Geography
- **Entry Origination Country:** First country a New Active User called from.
- **Entry Destination Country:** First country outside their own they call to. Users belong to one destination country.

### Channels
- **Acquisition Channel:** How a user was acquired (applies per product).
- **Origination Channel:** How a New User was first acquired.
- **Reactivation Channel:** How a Churned User came back.

### Calling-Specific
- **MXP:** In-app calls using data/wifi.
- **PSTN:** In-app calls using local phone lines.
- **SLN (Shared Local Number):** Saved local number tied to an international number.
- **LCR (Least Cost Routing):** Routing calls via the cheapest path.
- **MTR (Mobile Termination Rate):** Rate charged by the terminating operator.

### Financial
- **Burn-in:** Subscription charge/renewal transaction.
- **FX cost/revenue:** Difference between provider and charged exchange rate (MTU and Remittance).
- **TAF (Tell A Friend):** Referral program. TAF Cost = termination cost from free referral credit.
- **Welcome Offer (WO):** Offer given to new users.
